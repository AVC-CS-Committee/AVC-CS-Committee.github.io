# Getting back into Music Production by coding in Strudel
### by Maria Trezha Von Bargen

Personally and culturally music has been a significant part of my life. I can list my experience with music like playing the violin and piano, singing karaoke all the time with family, and being club president of the DJ club in highschool and producing really bad songs on garageband. So when a girl got whiff of Strudel. This programming language used to produce music and blend visualization into the code itself; a girl became interested, really interested, and went down the rabbit hole.

## Music and Me - My history with music

So culturally and ethnicity wise, I am half-filipino; half pinoy, and most of my childhood was spent in the Philippines. So you can understand why I am the way I am. I'm talking about hearing karaoke sessions wherever and whenever, everyone being forced to partake in cultural dances and performances, everyone being super talented and rhythmically articulate, it's actually kind of impressive and I appreciate it more now as an adult looking back. Back home I learned how to play the Violin and Piano, and it was something I enjoyed a lot and was known for. I was comfortable with the violin that I was able to play most songs by ear. Sadly I had to give that up when I moved to America back in 2014; left my violin and keyboard behind, and didn't touch music again until high school. High school was where I first learned what music production and DJ'ing is. The teacher in charge for robotics and engineering was also a DJ, and created a club to teach us the basics. We were called Scratch Crew, and I stayed in that club for all 4 years of high school. I learned how to "scratch" music for live sets, learned how to transition from song to song, learned how to slice and splice audio samples, and have produced some, questionable music on Garageband. There is exactly one piece of media from this era of my life that is public for everyone to see, which is a [chill hop song](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TEIJAJlh3qI) I made from slicing up old jazz songs. However, once I graduated, I didn't make any new music or mixes and totally left DJ'ing and producing behind for college.

## The Comeback - A rekindled interest as a young adult

When I got to college, I had a plan. Become a double major in Comp-Sci and Animation, so I can become a game developer. That was and still is my dream career and main plan. But this blog isn't about my journey figuring out what that dream is, but rather, rediscovering my interest in music production. Which I say, is productive towards game development since games need music and sound.

Unfortunately, I don't have the same access to the tools I had when I was in that high school club. My teacher provided us the mixing tables, the laptop, the speaker setup, and other weird electronic music instruments like a theramin. Back then I also had a mac, which had garageband and fl studio on it. I don't have the club or the mac, but I do have an awesome pc build. I just have to pick a software.

## What is Strudel? - No, seriously what is Strudel?

Not going to lie, it's hard to explain but once you see it, and get it, you get it.\
It's a "free, open-source, web-based live coding platform used to produce music in real time." The whole idea is that with strudel, you can code music in real-time. With one line of code, you can edit things like time signitures, type of instrument/sound being played, what kind of effects you can slap on, and most importantly, looping sounds. Because if you think about it, coding and music production aren't too different from each other in way looping is utilized.
